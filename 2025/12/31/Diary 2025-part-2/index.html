The Wilderness: A Road I Am Walking

Looking back, I realize
this is not my first time walking through the wilderness.

Over the past five years of constant movement,
I have repeatedly been led away
from places that felt familiar
and had already begun to feel stable.
Each time I thought I had finally settled,
life shifted again,
leading me into another unknown season.

At first, I did not understand why.
It felt as though I kept losing what I had built,
without being given anything to replace it.

Only later did I remember—
this path had been shown to me from the very beginning.

It was during my first year of internship.
In an ordinary moment,
God revealed an image that was both clear and exposing:
the wilderness is the place where I stand face to face with Him,
with nothing left to rely on.

I was not comforted by that image.
Instead, I was unsettled,
because I suddenly realized that
I did not love God.

I could not accept this truth immediately.
It was not a failure I could correct,
nor a confusion that could be soothed.
It simply existed,
leaving me with nowhere to hide.

I did not respond right away.
I did not change at once.
I carried this realization with me,
continuing my life while quietly trying
to avoid it,
to pretend it did not matter.

At that time,
I still had environments, roles, and a sense of direction
to rely on.
The wilderness felt like spiritual language,
not lived reality.

Five years later,
I find myself standing exactly where that image pointed.

I can no longer rely on familiar structures
to hold my sense of security,
nor define myself by roles I once carried.
The future feels unclear,
and control is no longer available.
In a very real sense,
I am left with nothing.

And it is here—
in this lack—
that I find myself once again,
and more honestly,
standing face to face with God.

This time,
I am no longer rushing to defend myself
or prove anything.
I have learned to stop
and admit that I cannot stand on my own.

Along this road,
my partner keeps reminding me
to move forward, not backward.

Not because the path ahead is clear,
but because returning to what is familiar
would only restore dependencies
that no longer sustain me.

I have begun to understand this journey
as a form of pilgrimage.
Like a traveler on a narrow road,
the burden remains,
the difficulty has not disappeared,
yet stopping is not an option.

Not because I am strong enough,
but because this road
was never meant to lead me back
to where I once stood.

In the wilderness,
I am learning not to ask whether I am ready,
but how to keep walking in the absence of certainty.
Not clinging to what once supported me,
but discovering—when support is gone—
who I am truly facing.

I still do not know where this path will lead.
But for now, I know this:
I am not lost.

I am being led forward,
face to face with God,
with nothing left to rely on.

旷野：我正在走的一段路

回头看，我才意识到，
这并不是我第一次走在旷野中。

在过去五年的漂泊经历里，
我一次又一次被带离熟悉、
也已经逐渐稳定下来的地方。
每当我以为终于站稳，
生活便再次发生变化，
把我带向下一个未知的阶段。

起初，我并不明白其中的意义。
我只觉得自己不断失去已经建立好的东西，
却没有得到可以替代它们的答案。

直到后来我才想起——
这条路，其实在一开始就已经向我显明过。

那是在我实习的第一年。
在一个并不特别的时刻，
上帝向我显明了一幅极其清晰、却也极其赤裸的画面：
旷野，是我在一无所有的情况下，
与祂面对面的地方。

那时的我，并没有被这个画面安慰。
相反，它让我震惊，
因为我突然意识到——
我并不爱神。

这个事实让我无法立刻接受。
它不像失败那样可以被修正，
也不像迷失那样可以被安抚。
它只是安静地存在着，
让我无处回避。

我没有立刻回应，
也没有马上改变。
我只是带着这个认知继续生活，
在很长一段时间里，
试图忽略它、绕开它，
假装它并不重要。

那时的我，
仍然拥有可以依靠的环境、身份与前路感。
“旷野”更像是一种属灵语言，
而不是我真实所站立的位置。

五年过去了。
当我再次回到那句话时，
我才发现自己正站在它所指向的地方。

如今的我，
已经无法再用熟悉的结构支撑安全感，
也不能再用既有的角色定义自己。
前路并不清晰，
未来也不再可控。
从某种意义上说，
我真的站在了“一无所有”的位置。

而正是在这样的状态里，
我再次、也是更真实地，
与上帝面对面。

这一次，我不再急着为自己辩解，
也不再试图证明什么。
我只是停下来，
承认我无法靠自己站稳。

而在这段路上，
我的伴侣不断提醒我：
要前进，而不是后退。

不是因为前方已经清楚，
而是因为退回熟悉之处，
只会让我再次依赖那些
已经无法支撑我的东西。

我开始意识到，
这条路更像是一段朝圣。
像天路上的行者一样，
负重仍在，
艰难并未减少，
但脚步不能停下。

不是因为我足够坚强，
而是因为这条路
从一开始就不是让我退回原处的。

在旷野中，
我逐渐不再问自己是否已经准备好，
而是学习在缺乏中继续前行。
不是抓紧所有还能抓住的东西，
而是在支撑被拿走时，
确认我真正面对的是谁。

我仍然不知道这条路会通向哪里。
但至少此刻，
我知道自己并没有迷路。

我正在一无所有之中，
与神面对面，
被带领着前行。
