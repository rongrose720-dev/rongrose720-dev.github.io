Moving Forward Amid Uncertainty

Just at this critical moment,
troubles also occurred at home and in the church.
I did not obtain clear answers from these events,
but they led me to a realization—
I care too much about outcomes.

This is not a judgment of others,
but an understanding of myself.

When I invest, wait, and endure,
I am hoping for a conclusion—
one that proves this road is right,
one that reassures me I have not chosen wrongly.
And when reality does not move in that direction,
I begin to lose certainty
and seriously consider giving up.

I realized that
I was not questioning whether God is real,
but whether this road is still worth walking
if it does not produce the outcome I expect.

In this struggle,
I began to see more clearly
what God truly values.

God does not prioritize results,
but God values whether a person understands
their own redemption before Him.
Saving souls is a responsibility,
but one must not lose
the salvation of being redeemed
in the process of carrying that responsibility.

I often think of Moses.
People may regret that he didn’t entered Canaan,
but God wanted him to understand:
your destination is not Canaan,
but eternity.

This realization slowly turned inward.
The path we have taken together,
perhaps truly involves being besieged from all sides.
The situation at home remains complicated,
and the reality has not improved immediately.
Yet this does not mean
the road has drifted from God’s will.

I do not need to fully understand every part of this path,
nor do I need to see the end.
What I can do
is choose, each day, to keep moving forward—
to rely on Him amid uncertainty.

Along the way,
I began to notice a different order.
No matter how family or church circumstances shift,
no matter how reality tests my capacity,
faith is not meant to immediately resolve problems.
It is the ability to keep walking
while the problems remain.

So, my partner and I learned to
support each other in our weaknesses.
In the midst of loneliness and pressure,
we kept reminding each other:
The journey still needs to go on,
but we will not be swallowed by fear and anxiety.

This path may be difficult,
and even raise doubts,
yet it is not aimless wandering.
It is a path that belongs to us-
and also a journey
walking with God.

不确定中的前行

正是在这个节骨眼上，
家里和教会也相继发生了变故。
这些事情并没有给我一个明确的答案，
却让我清楚地意识到一件事——
我太在乎结果了。

这不是对别人的判断，
而是对我自己的认识。

当我付出、等待、忍耐，
我其实一直在心里期待一个“结局”。
一个可以证明这条路是对的结局，
一个能让我安心、
让我确认自己没有走错的结果。
而当现实没有朝着我期待的方向发展时，
我开始不再确信，
也开始认真地想要放弃。

我发现，
我并不是在怀疑上帝是否真实，
而是在问自己：
如果没有我期待的结果，
这条路还值不值得继续走下去？

正是在这样的挣扎里，
我开始重新理解
神真正看重的是什么。

神看重的不是结果，
而是一个人是否真正明白
自己在祂面前的救赎问题。
拯救灵魂是责任，
但人不能因为责任，
而失去自己在神面前
被拯救、被更新的真实。

我常常想起摩西。
人会为他没有进入迦南而遗憾，
但神要人明白：
你的目的地不是迦南，
而是永恒。

这个认知慢慢落在我自己身上。
我和伴侣所走的这条路，
或许真的四面楚歌。
家里的情况依旧复杂，
现实并没有立刻好转。
但这并不意味着
这条路偏离了神的心意。

我开始明白——
不需要我完全理解它的每一个环节，
也不需要我看到终点。
我能做的，
只是每天选择继续前行，
在不确定中，选择依靠祂。

在这个过程中，
我开始看见一个新的秩序。
不论家庭和教会如何动荡，
不论现实怎样挑战我的能力，
信心并不是用来立刻解决问题的，
而是在问题依然存在时，
仍然能够向前。

于是，我和伴侣学会
在彼此的软弱中互相托住。
在孤独与压力之中，
一次次提醒对方：
路还需要继续走，
但我们不必被恐惧和焦虑吞没。

这条路或许艰难，
甚至令人怀疑，
却并非毫无方向的漂流。
它是一段属于我们的路，
也是一段
与神同行的旅程。
