Suffering, Hope, Grace, and Glory

During this season,
that I thought I was simply carrying external pressure.
Only later did I realize
what I truly could not bear
was that God was not acting
according to my understanding.

I had been interpreting this road
through a human perspective.
I assumed obedience should bring stability,
and trust should result in forward movement.
When everything became difficult, stagnant,
or appeared to make no progress,
I began to lose certainty—
and I seriously considered giving up.

Self-pity, regret,
and resentment toward God slowly surfaced.
I was no longer just asking “why,”
but quietly weighing
whether this road was still worth walking.

The difficulty of walking this path in the United States
forced me to face a hard truth:
I no longer had the ability
to move forward based on my own understanding.

Under this condition,
God did not rush to remove my problems.
Instead,
He prepared a quiet space—
not to help me escape reality,
but to allow me to stop
before I became weary completely,
and to relearn what it meant to rely on God.

It was during this same season
that I began reading “Be Hopeful”.
At first, I did not come with expectation.
I was simply looking for something
that would keep me from letting go entirely.

Through the reading,
I finally began to understand
what people refer to as suffering.
It was no longer a spiritual phrase
or distant comfort,
but a very real condition—
the place where control and explanation collapse,
and a person is brought before God.

I began to understand that:
suffering is not a synonym for things not going well,
nor is it proof of deviating from God’s will.
On the contrary,
it is often God’s most direct,
and also most incomprehensible, way of shaping us.

This also enabled me to truly understand for the first time
why the Bible places suffering
on the same level as glory.

Glory is not
the reward that emerges after the end of suffering.
Rather, it is revealed in the midst of suffering,
when people no longer measure this path by
success, smoothness, efficiency,
but rather by their own nature,
God’s character begins to manifest itself in them.

The phrase “suffering is glory”
was no longer empty.
It did not require positivity
or force gratitude.
It simply showed me
that this road had not been wasted.

I began to loosen my grip
on human explanations,
and to release the assumption that
a different choice would have guaranteed an easier life.

Hope did not arrive after problems disappeared.
It returned quietly
while the problems remained—
in the fact that I was not consumed by despair.

Grace did not prevent me from taking this path,
but it allowed me to remain on this path
without completely collapsing.

And glory no longer feels like a distant destination.
Rather, it is when I am still weak,
still facing the pressures of reality,
still unable to see the path ahead,
yet still, God has not abandoned me.

The problems remain.
The road is still difficult.
I still feel uncertain at times.

But now I am no longer resenting God
because I did not understand Him.
I am learning to end
the mindset that once caused me
to lose certainty
and almost give up.

苦难、盼望、恩典与荣耀

在这一段时间里，
我以为自己是在承受环境的压力，
后来才发现，
我真正无法承受的，
是上帝没有按照我所理解的方式行事。

我一直用一种“人的思维方式”
来理解这条路。
我以为顺服应该带来稳定，
信靠应该换来前进的能力。
而当一切变得艰难、停滞，
甚至看起来毫无进展时，
我开始不再确信，
也开始认真地想要放弃。

自怨自艾、后悔，
和对上帝的埋怨慢慢浮现。
我不再只是问“为什么”，
而是在心里反复衡量：
这条路是否还值得继续走下去。

在美国这条路上的艰难，
让我不得不面对一个事实：
我已经没有办法
靠自己的理解继续前进。

正是在这样的状态里，
上帝没有急着为我挪去问题，
却为我预备了一个安静的环境。
不是为了让我逃离现实，
而是让我停下来，
不再继续消耗自己，
开始重新学习依靠。

也是在这一段时间，
我开始读《Be Hopeful》。
起初，我并不是带着期待，
只是想抓住一点
不至于让我彻底放弃的东西。

但正是在阅读中，
我第一次真正理解了
人们口中所说的 suffering。
它不再是一句属灵语言，
也不是旁观者的安慰，
而是一个极其真实的状态——
人在失去掌控、
失去解释能力的时候，
被带到上帝面前。

我开始明白，
suffering 并不是
事情不顺利的代名词，
也不是偏离上帝旨意的证明。
相反，
它往往是上帝最直接、
也最不被人理解的塑造方式。

这也让我第一次真正理解
为什么圣经会把 suffering
与 glory 放在同一条线上。

荣耀并不是
苦难结束之后才出现的奖励，
而是在苦难之中，
当人不再用
成功、顺利、效率
来衡量这条路时，
上帝的性情开始在人身上被显明。

“suffering is glory”
这句话不再是一句空话。
它不要求我立刻积极，
也不逼我马上感恩。
它只是让我意识到——
这条路并没有被浪费。

我开始停止用人的逻辑
评判自己所走的路，
也慢慢放下
“如果当初选择不同，
是不是就不会这么难”的假设。

盼望并不是在问题解决之后才出现，
而是在问题依旧存在、
我却没有被绝望吞没的时候，
被重新点燃的。

恩典并没有让我绕开这条路，
却让我在这条路上
不再完全崩塌。

而荣耀，
不再只是一个遥远的终点，
而是在我仍然软弱、
仍然面对现实压力、
仍然看不清前路的时候，
我却没有被放弃。

问题依旧存在。
这条路仍然艰难。
我也仍然会感到不安。

但至少现在，
我不再因为不明白
而埋怨上帝，
而是在学习放下
那套曾经让我不确信、
也几乎让我放弃的思维方式。
